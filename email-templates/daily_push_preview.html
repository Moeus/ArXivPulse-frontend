Scaling Laws for Reward Model Overoptimization in Direct Alignment Algorithms

While reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) is central to aligning large language models, reward overoptimization — where the model exploits reward model weaknesses — poses a significant challenge. We study how overoptimization scales with model size, dataset size, and RL policy constraints, analyzing the phenomenon across both proximal policy optimization and direct alignment algorithms such as DPO.

Rafael Rafailov, Archit Sharma, Eric Mitchell, Christopher D. Manning, Stefano Ermon, Chelsea Finn

cs.CV CVPR 2026 2026-03-28

DINOv3: Self-Supervised Vision Transformers with Enhanced Spatial Reasoning

We present DINOv3, a self-supervised vision transformer that incorporates a novel spatial consistency objective to address the limited spatial reasoning capabilities of prior DINO-family models. By encouraging patch-level feature alignment across multi-scale views and introducing a learnable positional prototype mechanism, DINOv3 achieves state-of-the-art performance on dense prediction tasks while retaining strong transferability to classification benchmarks.

Mathilde Caron, Hugo Touvron, Ishan Misra, Hervé Jégou, Julien Mairal, Piotr Bojanowski, Armand Joulin

cs.AI Nature Machine Intelligence 2026-03-25

Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models

We explore how generating a chain of thought — a series of intermediate reasoning steps — significantly improves the ability of large language models to perform complex reasoning. In a series of experiments on arithmetic, commonsense, and symbolic reasoning tasks, we find that chain-of-thought prompting substantially outperforms standard prompting, with GPT-scale models showing the largest gains.

Jason Wei, Xuezhi Wang, Dale Schuurmans, Maarten Bosma, Brian Ichter, Fei Xia, Ed Chi, Quoc Le, Denny Zhou
